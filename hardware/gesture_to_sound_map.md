# Gesture → Acoustic Response Map

## Definition

This document defines how human physical interaction produces sound in Spectral String.

No electronics are involved in this mapping.

---

## 1. Input Domain (Human Gesture)

Primary gestures:

- pluck
- strike
- slide
- press
- release
- optional bowing

Each gesture is continuous, not discrete.

---

## 2. Physical Variables

Sound is determined by:

- force magnitude
- contact position along string
- velocity of excitation
- angle of interaction
- duration of contact

---

## 3. Output Domain (Acoustic Response)

Gestures produce:

- amplitude envelope
- harmonic excitation spectrum
- decay curve behavior
- resonance coupling across body

---

## 4. Mapping Principle

There is no digital translation layer.

The mapping is physical:

gesture → energy transfer → structural response → sound

---

## 5. Continuity Rule

There are no triggers, notes, or events.

All response is continuous in time and space.

---

## 6. Core Statement

The performer does not trigger sound.

The performer shapes physical energy distribution in a resonant system.
