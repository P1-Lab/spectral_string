# Mechanical Voice Engine

## Definition

The Mechanical Voice Engine is the physical system by which Spectral String produces sound.

It is not software. It is the instrument itself.

---

## 1. Sound Generation Chain

Sound emerges through:

1. String excitation (gesture input)
2. Energy transfer through bridge system
3. Structural resonance of body
4. Air displacement from enclosure
5. Radiated acoustic field

No step requires electronics.

---

## 2. Voice Determinants

Instrument “voice” is defined by:

- string tension profile
- material stiffness of body
- bridge coupling geometry
- cavity resonance characteristics
- mass distribution across structure

---

## 3. Nonlinear Behavior

The system is not linear or discrete:

- small gesture changes produce continuous variation
- resonance interacts with excitation position
- harmonic structure evolves dynamically over time

---

## 4. Physical Stability Requirement

For consistent voice behavior:

- mechanical rigidity must be preserved
- coupling surfaces must remain fixed
- energy loss paths must be controlled

---

## 5. Core Principle

The instrument does not simulate voice.

It *is* the voice.
