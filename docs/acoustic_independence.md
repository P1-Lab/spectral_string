# Acoustic Independence Principle

## Definition

Spectral String is acoustically complete without any electronic system.

---

## 1. Unplugged Operation

In unplugged mode, the instrument functions as:

- a tensioned string system
- a resonant body structure
- a mechanically tuned harmonic device

Sound is produced entirely through physical vibration.

---

## 2. What Exists Without Electronics

The following remain fully functional:

- pitch generation (string length + tension)
- harmonic structure (physical overtones)
- dynamic response (gesture interaction)
- timbral variation (contact position + force)

---

## 3. What Electronics Do NOT Do

Electronics do not:

- generate sound
- define pitch
- replace acoustic resonance
- correct instability in physical behavior

---

## 4. Role of Electronics (Optional Layer)

When present, electronics only:

- measure vibration
- model spectral behavior
- provide tuning references
- analyze gesture input

They are observational, not generative.

---

## 5. Core Statement

The instrument is not “powered.”

It is played.
