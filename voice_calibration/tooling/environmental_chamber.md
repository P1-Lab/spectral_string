# Environmental Chamber Specification

## Purpose

The environmental chamber controls external variables that affect acoustic and mechanical behavior of the instrument system.

It ensures repeatability across environmental conditions.

---

## 1. Controlled Parameters

### Humidity
- Range: 30% – 70% RH
- Stability: ±2% RH

Purpose:
- controls wood expansion behavior
- stabilizes damping characteristics

---

### Temperature
- Range: 15°C – 30°C
- Stability: ±0.5°C

Purpose:
- prevents drift in material stiffness
- stabilizes electronic component behavior

---

### Air Pressure / Density
- monitored for acoustic consistency
- used for correction normalization only

---

## 2. Acoustic Isolation

- chamber must be acoustically damped
- external vibration isolation required
- no reflective standing wave contamination

---

## 3. Measurement Window Control

- fixed excitation-to-measurement timing
- consistent settling time per test
- repeatable environmental stabilization delay

---

## Summary

The environmental chamber ensures that Voice calibration reflects the instrument—not the room.
